FAQ

How does Class 5 work?

Start by entering your basic information into your user profile and inputting between 2-6 liked courses, 2-6 disliked courses, and your favorite “fifth” course (see “What is a favorite ‘fifth’ course?” below). After populating your profile, head to the Dashboard to get a curated list of suggestions based on your preferences. Under “Course Info”, you can peruse additional course information and helpful insights to see whether a suggestion is right for you.


How does Class 5 get suggested courses?

After providing your course preferences, you're given suggestions based on preferences of similar users. For example, if you like COS 333, you’re more likely to be suggested courses of users who also liked COS 333. Our algorithm is a bit more complicated, but that’s the gist of it.


What is a favorite “fifth” course?

This could be a course you found fun, easy, or enjoyed in some way (maybe even unexpectedly!). Often, a “fifth” course has nothing to do with your academic path at Princeton.


What are “Filters”?

Need to quickly find a 5th course? Looking for which courses to add to your bucket list or which courses to avoid? Under “Filters”, click “Most Liked Courses,” “Most Disliked Courses,” or “Top Fifth Courses” to see a list of the 50 most popular Liked, Disliked or Fifth courses among all Class 5 users.


How can I get new suggestions?

“Disliking” a suggestion with the “x” button instantly eliminates it from your list of suggestions and repopulates the list with an additional suggestion. You can get up to 30 new suggestions after the 10 initially provided. If you run out of suggestions, refresh the page to get brand new suggestions.


I removed a suggestion by accident, can I get it back?

Absolutely! Head to the “Profile” page to remove any disliked suggestion, or clear all of your disliked suggestions. After doing so, remember to save your changes!
